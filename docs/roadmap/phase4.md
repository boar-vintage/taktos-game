PHASE_4_SOCIAL_PRESENCE.md — Social Presence & Live Interaction Layer

Overview

Phase 4 is where Taktos stops feeling like a product
and starts feeling like a world.

Up to this point:
	•	Users can explore
	•	Users can save and drop resumes
	•	Employers can unlock candidates

But the experience is still mostly solo.

Phase 4 introduces:

Real people, in real time, sharing the same space

⸻

Core Goal

Make Taktos feel:
	•	Alive
	•	Social
	•	Serendipitous

Without:
	•	Chat systems
	•	Moderation complexity
	•	Noise

⸻

Key System: Synchronous Presence

Users can now:
	•	See other users walking around
	•	See employers in the world
	•	Interact through lightweight, structured communication

⸻

Street Becomes Multiplayer

Before Phase 4
	•	Single-player exploration
	•	Static environment

After Phase 4
	•	Shared spaces (rooms)
	•	Multiple avatars (5–20 per instance)
	•	Real-time movement

⸻

Avatar System

Each user is represented by:
	•	8-bit isometric avatar
	•	Walk animation
	•	Idle animation

Employers:
	•	Have a visible 🏳 flag indicator
	•	Slight visual distinction (badge, clothing detail)

⸻

Interaction System (“Tak Tak”)

Design Principle

No free text.

All interactions are:
	•	Intent-driven
	•	Structured
	•	Safe

⸻

Flow
	1.	User taps another user
	2.	Prompt appears:
	•	“Tak?”
	3.	Other user responds:
	•	“Tak.”

Then both see a menu of options:

Examples:
	•	“Looking for retail work”
	•	“Know any places hiring?”
	•	“Saved any good spots?”
	•	“New to this area”

Responses return:
	•	Storefront cards
	•	Recommendations
	•	Signals (e.g. “this place is good”)

⸻

Employer Interactions

When interacting with employer avatars:

Options:
	•	“Are you hiring?”
	•	“I dropped a resume”
	•	“What roles do you need?”

Employer responds via:
	•	“Yes, hiring evenings”
	•	“Resume received”
	•	“Check storefront”
	•	“Not hiring yet”

⸻

No Chat Policy

There is:
	•	No messaging system
	•	No inbox
	•	No free-form typing

This avoids:
	•	Moderation burden
	•	Spam
	•	Harassment

⸻

Presence Signals

To reinforce social energy:
	•	“X people here now”
	•	“Y employers nearby”

These are:
	•	Subtle
	•	Informational
	•	Not gamified

⸻

Instance Design

To maintain quality:
	•	Each street = small room (5–20 users)
	•	Automatic sharding
	•	Seamless transitions between areas

Goals:
	•	Avoid overcrowding
	•	Maintain visibility
	•	Keep interactions meaningful

⸻

Privacy & Control

Users can:
	•	Toggle visibility (walkable vs private)
	•	Disable interactions
	•	Limit exposure

Employers must:
	•	Be verified before appearing as avatars
	•	Have claimed storefront

⸻

Behavioral Shift

Before Phase 4:
	•	Users explore places

After Phase 4:
	•	Users explore people + places

⸻

Social Discovery Loop

Example:

User:
	•	Sees another job seeker
	•	“Tak tak”
	•	Gets recommendation

→ Visits storefront
→ Saves it
→ Drops resume

Discovery becomes:
Human-driven, not search-driven

⸻

Key Metrics
	•	Concurrent users per room
	•	Interaction rate (“tak tak” usage)
	•	Store visits from social interactions
	•	Session duration

⸻

Risks

1. Dead Streets

No users → no magic

Solution:
	•	Seed with NPCs
	•	Show presence indicators

⸻

2. Interaction Spam

Too many taps

Solution:
	•	Cooldowns
	•	“Busy” indicators

⸻

3. Creepiness

Overexposure of users

Solution:
	•	No direct messaging
	•	Controlled identity visibility
	•	Opt-out controls

⸻

Strategic Insight

This phase creates:
	•	Emotional connection
	•	Organic discovery
	•	Network effects

Without:
	•	Social media toxicity

⸻

Outcome

Phase 4 transforms Taktos into:

A shared, social environment for discovering work

This is the foundation for:
	•	Habit loops (Phase 6)
	•	Creator worlds (Phase 7)

⸻

Guiding Principle

Work is social.
Taktos makes it visible.—

Next would be Phase 3 (Unlock marketplace + monetization) — where the business model comes online.

# PHASE_5_MULTI_CITY.md — Multi-City Expansion & Market Validation

## Overview

Phase 5 proves that Taktos is not a one-off experience.

It validates that:
- The model works across different types of labor markets
- The system is portable
- The world can scale beyond a single city

---

## Core Goal

> Build a repeatable playbook for launching new “worlds”

And prove:

- Retail works  
- Hospitality works  
- Tech works  
- Remote works  

---

## Expansion Strategy

Taktos launches with three distinct market types:

### 1. Austin (Retail / Food Focus)

Austin

Purpose:
- High density of SMB employers
- Fast hiring cycles
- Strong “work vibe” culture

What it tests:
- Wishlist behavior for physical locations  
- Resume drop without urgency  
- Employer claim + unlock in SMB segment  

---

### 2. San Diego (Retail / Hospitality / Tourism)

San Diego

Purpose:
- Service industry hiring
- Seasonal / flexible labor
- Tourism-driven businesses

What it tests:
- High turnover hiring dynamics  
- Employer responsiveness  
- Event-driven hiring (Phase 6 tie-in)  

---

### 3. Remote (Tech Layer)

Non-geographic “world”

Purpose:
- Structured job discovery
- Long-cycle hiring
- High-value candidates

What it tests:
- Wishlist for aspirational companies  
- Skill-based matching  
- Non-location-based navigation  

---

## World Design Differences

Each market behaves differently:

| Market | Structure | Behavior |
|------|--------|---------|
| Austin | Geographic | Walk + discover |
| San Diego | Geographic | Walk + high churn hiring |
| Remote | Non-geographic | Browse + filter |

---

## Data Strategy

### Austin / San Diego
- API-driven (Google Places / Yelp)
- High freshness required
- Focus on:
  - Category
  - Location
  - Visual identity

### Remote
- Structured datasets (e.g. Crunchbase)
- Focus on:
  - Industry
  - Company size
  - Remote-friendly signals

---

## City Launch Playbook

For each new city:

### Step 1 — Seed Supply
- Import 10k–20k businesses
- Ensure storefront density

---

### Step 2 — Activate Demand
- Invite early job seekers
- Encourage wishlist + resume drops

---

### Step 3 — Activate Employers
- Target early adopters
- Push claim flow
- Highlight latent demand

---

### Step 4 — Measure Loop

Validate:
- Wishlist → resume → unlock → contact

---

## Portability Requirements

To scale cities efficiently:

- Unified business schema
- Modular world generation (tile-based)
- Standard storefront system
- Configurable categories per city

---

## Key Metrics

Per city:

- # of businesses seeded  
- % claimed  
- Wishlists per user  
- Resume drops per business  
- Employer unlock rate  
- Time spent in world  

---

## Success Criteria

Phase 5 is successful if:

- At least 2 city types show strong engagement
- Employer unlocks occur in multiple markets
- Users behave consistently across cities
- Launching a new city is repeatable

---

## Risks

### 1. Fragmentation
Too many cities → low density

Solution:
- Limit active cities early
- Ensure each feels alive

---

### 2. Data Quality
Poor business data → bad experience

Solution:
- Prioritize accuracy over completeness
- Focus on visible storefront quality

---

### 3. Behavior Mismatch
What works in retail may not work in tech

Solution:
- Treat each world differently
- Do not force uniform UX

---

## Strategic Insight

Taktos is not:

“One product deployed everywhere”

It is:

> A system for building worlds tailored to different labor markets

---

## Outcome

Phase 5 proves:

- Taktos can scale across geographies and industries  
- The model is adaptable  
- The foundation exists for creator-built worlds (Phase 7)

# PHASE_6_DENSITY_EVENTS.md — Density, Habit, and Live Energy

## Overview

Phase 6 transforms Taktos from a functional product into a living world with energy.

Up to this point:
- Users can explore
- Employers can unlock candidates
- The system works

But it may still feel:
- Quiet
- Occasional
- Passive

Phase 6 solves:

> “Why should I come back right now?”

---

## Core Goal

Create time-based urgency and social gravity.

Make Taktos feel:
- Alive at certain times
- Worth checking daily
- Better with more people

---

## Key Systems

### 1. Live Density System

Show users that the world is active.

Examples:
- “12 people on South Congress right now”
- “3 employers nearby”
- “Hiring active in this district”

Effects:
- Encourages entry
- Reduces “dead world” feeling
- Creates curiosity

---

### 2. Micro-Instance Sharding

To maintain quality:

- Each street = small room (5–20 users)
- Auto-balance density
- Avoid overcrowding

Users feel:
- Presence
- Not chaos

---

### 3. NPC / Ghost Layer

To prevent emptiness:

- Simulated walkers (NPCs)
- “Ghost trails” (recent activity)
- Passive signals:
  - “5 visited recently”
  - “Someone just saved this”

Goal:
Always feel like something is happening.

---

## Event System

### 1. Scheduled Events

Examples:

- “Hiring Night — Austin (7–9pm)”
- “Brunch Rush — Cafés Hiring Now”
- “Remote Tech Drop — New Roles Live”

Features:
- Time-bound
- Highlighted districts
- Increased employer presence

---

### 2. Employer Participation

Employers can:
- Opt into events
- Mark “active hiring”
- Appear more prominently

Visual:
- Glow on storefront
- Stronger employer flag
- Increased visibility

---

### 3. User Notifications

Push notifications:

- “3 places you saved are active now”
- “Employers are online near you”
- “Hiring event starting in 10 min”

Goal:
Drive synchronous return behavior.

---

## Social Amplification

### 1. Crowd Signals

Light social proof:

- “8 people here now”
- “4 people saved this today”
- “Trending in this district”

Avoid:
- Competitive pressure
- Over-gamification

---

### 2. Serendipity Engine

Users discover via:

- Other users
- Shared recommendations (“try this place”)
- Movement patterns

Not:
Search.

---

## Employer Dynamics

During events:

Employers:
- Log in as avatars
- Respond to “tak tak”
- Indicate hiring status

This creates:

> Live hiring moments

Equivalent of:
A job fair, but ambient and distributed.

---

## Behavior Shift

Before Phase 6:
- Users explore occasionally

After Phase 6:
- Users return at specific times
- Users expect activity windows
- Employers show up intentionally

---

## Metrics of Success

- Concurrent users per district
- Event participation rate
- Return frequency (DAU/WAU)
- Session length during events
- Employer online presence rate

---

## Risks

### 1. Empty Events
If no one shows:
- Kills trust

Solution:
- Seed early events
- Ensure minimum density

---

### 2. Overcrowding
Too many users:
- Noise
- Confusion

Solution:
- Shard aggressively

---

### 3. Notification Fatigue
Too many pushes:
- Users churn

Solution:
- Tie notifications to user intent
- Only notify for saved businesses

---

## Guiding Principle

> Presence creates presence.

The system should always answer:

“Why now?”

---

## Outcome

Phase 6 creates:

- Habit loops
- Social energy
- Time-based engagement

This is the bridge from:
Product → Platform

Because once people show up regularly,
Creators (Phase 7) have something to build on.
